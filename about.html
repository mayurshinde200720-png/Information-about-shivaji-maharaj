About Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Early Life & Background

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was a legendary Indian warrior king and the founder of the Maratha Empire. He was born at the Shivneri Fort near Pune. From a young age, he showed exceptional leadership, courage, and strategic thinking.

Guided by his mother Jijabai and mentor Dadaji Kondadev, Shivaji Maharaj developed a strong sense of justice and the dream of establishing a Swarajya — a self-ruled kingdom for the people.

Leadership & Achievements

He built a powerful navy, reformed military tactics, and introduced a progressive administrative system. His use of guerrilla warfare, innovative war strategies, and construction of strong forts across the Western Ghats made him one of the greatest kings in Indian history.

Coronation

On 6 June 1674, Shivaji Maharaj was crowned as the Chhatrapati at Raigad Fort — marking the formal beginning of the Maratha Empire.
